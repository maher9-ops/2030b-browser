## PART 0 — DECLARATION AND CHARACTER OF THIS INSTRUMENT

### 0.1 Nature of the Document

Maher's License ("this License", "ML-1.1", or "the License") is a single legal instrument that performs three distinct functions. First, it is a **direct license** capable of governing software, source code, documentation, datasets, designs, and other expressive or functional works ("the Work"). Second, it is an **ethical-source license**, meaning it places conditions on the *use* of the Work that go beyond the conditions found in conventional permissive or copyleft licenses. Third, it is a **meta-license**: a top-level framework that defines an objective rubric by which *other* licenses may be evaluated and classified as Compatible, Conditionally Compatible, or Incompatible with the ethical foundation set out herein.

This three-fold character is deliberate. The drafters recognize that the global software-licensing ecosystem already contains many well-drafted licenses, and that proliferation of single-purpose licenses imposes real costs in legal-review burden, compatibility uncertainty, and tooling complexity. ML-1.1 does not seek merely to add one more license. It seeks to provide a *normative layer above* the landscape — a principled standard against which existing and future licenses can be measured — while remaining usable as a working license in its own right.

### 0.2 Honest Labeling (a requirement, not a disclaimer)

In keeping with the principle of **Sidq** (honesty and truthfulness), foundational to this instrument, the drafters state plainly: **this License is not an "Open Source" license as defined by the Open Source Initiative ("OSI"), and is not a "Free Software" license as defined by the Free Software Foundation ("FSF").** It is not, and is not intended to be, approved by either body. The reason is structural and disclosed openly: this License imposes conditions on the fields and purposes of use of the Work (see Part C), and both the Open Source Definition (criteria 5 and 6) and the Free Software Definition (Freedom 0) prohibit licenses that restrict use by persons, groups, or fields of endeavor. A license that conditions use on ethical compliance cannot satisfy those criteria.

The drafters consider this incompatibility a feature, not a defect. Concealing it — by marketing the License as "open source" when it is not — would itself violate **Sidq**, and be self-contradictory. Any party who reproduces, references, or relies upon this License must describe it accurately as an **ethical-source license** and a **meta-license**, and must not represent it as OSI-approved or FSF-approved unless and until such approval is granted in writing by the respective body.

### 0.3 Source-Available and Ethical-Source Classification

ML-1.1 occupies the category of **source-available, ethical-source licenses**, distinguished by (a) its explicit grounding in a named ethical-legal tradition, and (b) its meta-licensing function. The License preserves core liberties associated with open collaboration — the freedom to read, study, modify, and redistribute the Source Form of the Work — subject only to the Ethical-Use Conditions of Part C and the reciprocity conditions of Part B. The intent is that the License be *maximally permissive within ethical bounds*: every freedom that can be granted without enabling clear harm or dishonesty is granted.

### 0.4 The Self-Hosted Registry Model

This License is published and maintained through a self-hosted registry operated by or on behalf of the License Steward (the "Registry"), which serves as the canonical source of the License text, the authoritative version history, the publication venue for Compatibility Determinations under Part D, and the public list of evaluated licenses with their recorded advantages and disadvantages. Because the License is not dependent upon approval by any external standards body, the Registry — and not OSI, SPDX, or any third party — is the governing authority for the interpretation, versioning, and compatibility administration of this License.

## PART 1 — PREAMBLE AND ETHICAL-LEGAL FOUNDATION

### 1.1 Purpose of the Preamble

This Preamble is an integral and operative part of the License, not mere recital. Where any provision is ambiguous, the ambiguity shall be resolved in the manner most consistent with the principles in this Part, per §11 (Interpretation). The Foundational Principles are simultaneously the *spirit* and the *interpretive key* of the instrument.

### 1.2 The Four Foundational Principles

This License rests on four principles drawn from the Islamic ethical-legal tradition, presented as universal moral norms intelligible to persons of any faith or none. The drafters require no particular belief; they require only conduct consistent with these norms.

**(a) Amanah — Trustworthiness and Faithful Stewardship.** That which is held in trust, and the duty to discharge it faithfully. It governs the relationship between creators, maintainers, and users, and establishes that software and data placed in one's care — credentials, personal information, funds, reliance — must be safeguarded and not betrayed.

**(b) Sidq — Honesty and Truthfulness.** Truthfulness in word and representation: accurate attribution, honest description of modifications, truthful labeling of the License's own character, and the prohibition of using the Work to deceive, defraud, or misinform.

**(c) Adl — Justice, Fairness, and Proportionality.** Justice and giving each party its due: balanced terms between licensor and licensee, proportionate (not punitive) remedies, reasonable cure periods, and no unjust structural advantage arising from the License's mere operation.

**(d) Shafafiyah — Transparency and Openness.** Openness and the absence of concealment: source availability for lawful recipients, plainly stated terms, and reasoned rather than arbitrary Compatibility Determinations.

### 1.3 Subsidiary Ethical Norms

**(i) Prevention of Darar (harm).** The maxim *lā ḍarar wa-lā ḍirār* — "neither harming nor reciprocating harm" — grounds the ethical-use conditions and the proportionality of enforcement.

**(ii) Prohibition of Zulm (oppression and exploitation).** The Work may not be an instrument of oppression — forced labor, trafficking, unlawful discrimination, or systematic exploitation of vulnerable persons.

**(iii) Avoidance of Gharar (excessive uncertainty/deception in dealings).** The Work may not construct dealings whose essential terms are concealed from a counterparty so as to deprive them of informed consent.

**(iv) Avoidance of Riba (usurious exploitation).** *[Revised — Critique 3.]* *Riba* denotes unjust, exploitative gain extracted from another through the structure of a dealing, classically associated with usury. The drafters recognize that this norm applies to software only *indirectly and narrowly*. For the avoidance of all doubt, and because this License is intended to be usable by financial, monetary, commercial, and economic systems of every kind:

> This norm is an **interpretive value only**. It does **not** prohibit, condition, or disfavor: the design, operation, or distribution of currencies, ledgers, payment systems, lending or credit facilities, interest- or yield-bearing instruments, demurrage or negative-rate mechanisms, exchanges, markets, or any other lawful financial or economic functionality. It does **not** make the lawfulness of any financial activity a condition of the license grant under Part C, except to the limited and specific extent expressly set out in §C.8. The presence of this norm shall never be construed to render a financial Work, or its ordinary lawful use, non-compliant with this License. Any doubt as to whether a financial use is permitted shall be resolved **in favor of permission.**

### 1.4 Universality and Non-Coercion of Belief

Per the principle *lā ikrāha fī al-dīn* ("there is no compulsion in religion"), nothing in this License requires any user to adopt or profess any religion. The principles are invoked as a coherent, named *ethical-legal source* for the License's conditions, in the same manner that other ethical-source licenses invoke international human-rights instruments. Compliance is a matter of *conduct*, not belief.

### 1.5 Relationship to Secular and International Law; Reinforcing Character

*[Revised — Critique 1.]* This License is intended to operate consistently with, and as a supplement to, applicable secular and international law, including internationally recognized human-rights instruments. The drafters state expressly and as a matter of honest construction (*Sidq*):

> The Ethical-Use Conditions of Part C are designed primarily as a **reinforcing and remedial layer**. Where the conduct they prohibit is already unlawful under applicable law, the Conditions reinforce that law and supply the Licensor with an independent contractual and copyright-based hook (termination and scope-of-grant) that does not depend on the existence of new prohibitions. Where applicable law is silent, the Conditions operate as ordinary contractual conditions to the extent permitted by law, and **no greater enforceability is claimed for them than the law of the relevant forum will grant.** The drafters do not represent that Part C creates legally novel prohibitions where law is silent; they represent only that Part C gives the Licensor a lawful basis to withdraw the grant from a user who engages in the prohibited conduct. Persons relying on this License for the protection of systems of value or consequence are advised that **structural and technical safeguards, not this License, are the primary line of protection**, and that this License is the fallback that operates when such safeguards are absent, bypassed, or insufficient.

Where applicable law *compels* conduct this License would otherwise prohibit, §12 governs. The License does not override mandatory law nor authorize any unlawful act.

### 2.1 Construction

### 2.2 Defined Terms

**"The Work"**, **"Source Form"**, **"Object Form"**, **"Licensor"**, **"You"/"Your"**, **"Contribution"**, **"Contributor"**, **"Derivative"**, **"Distribute"**, **"Larger Work"**, **"Subject License"**, **"Compatibility Determination"**, **"License Steward"**, **"Registry"**, **"Ethical-Use Conditions"**, and **"Foundational Principles"** carry the meanings given to them in Version 1.0 of this License, which are incorporated here unchanged. *(For a self-contained publication, the Registry edition of ML-1.1 reproduces the full Version 1.0 definitions verbatim at this location.)*

**New definitions added in Version 1.1** *[Critique 2]*:

**"Network-Facing Work"** means a Work, or the modified portion of a Work, that is made available to remote users primarily by allowing them to interact with its functionality over a computer network (including as a hosted, multi-tenant, or "as-a-service" deployment), as opposed to being delivered to those users as a copy they execute themselves. A Work is a Network-Facing Work *only* where, in its actual deployment, remote interaction with its functionality is the principal means by which value is delivered to those users.

**"Conveyed Copy Work"** means a Work whose functionality is delivered principally by providing recipients with a copy they themselves run, whether locally, on their own infrastructure, or on infrastructure they control. A Work may be a Network-Facing Work as to some deployments and a Conveyed Copy Work as to others; the classification is determined per deployment.

**"Corresponding Network Source"** means the complete Source Form of the running version of a Network-Facing Work as actually deployed, including modifications, in the preferred form for making modifications.

**"Financial Functionality"** *[Critique 3]* means any feature of a Work pertaining to the creation, issuance, transfer, custody, exchange, valuation, lending, borrowing, yield, settlement, or accounting of currency, value, credit, or financial instruments of any kind.

**B.6.2 Obligation.** Where You operate a Network-Facing Work that You have modified, and make its functionality available to remote users over a network, You must offer those remote users access to the **Corresponding Network Source** of the version as actually deployed, under this License, by a means no more burdensome than the means by which You provide the functionality (for example, a conspicuous link from the interface or an accessible, documented endpoint). **B.6.5 Interaction with Financial Functionality.** For the avoidance of doubt, the obligation in B.6.2 concerns availability of *source code*. It does not require disclosure of operational secrets, private keys, user data, or other confidential operational material whose disclosure would itself create harm (*Darar*) or breach a trust (*Amanah*); the obligation is satisfied by providing the Corresponding Network Source with such genuinely sensitive operational secrets redacted, provided the redaction does not defeat the recipient's ability to study, build, and run an equivalent version.

## PART C — ETHICAL-USE CONDITIONS

### C.0 Legal Character of This Part — *[New, Critique 1]*

This Part is to be read subject to §1.5. The Conditions in this Part are framed as **conditions of the license grant** (scope-of-grant limitations), and breach of a Condition therefore places the offending use outside the scope of the rights granted in Part A. The drafters adopt this framing because a use falling outside the scope of a copyright grant is reachable as infringement, whereas a mere covenant may not be. However, the drafters expressly acknowledge, consistent with *Sidq*:

> Several Conditions in this Part prohibit conduct (such as fraud, trafficking, or unlawful harm) that is *already unlawful* under most legal systems. As to such conduct, this Part is **declaratory and reinforcing**: its operative legal effect is to give the Licensor a basis to **terminate the grant** (§9) and to characterize continued use as outside the scope of the license, **not** to create a new substantive prohibition that exists only by virtue of this License. The drafters make no representation that a court will treat any Condition as enforceable beyond what the law of the forum independently allows, and the validity of the remainder of the License does not depend on any particular Condition being independently enforceable (see §C.7 and §14).

### C.1 Status of the Conditions — *[Revised, Critique 1]*

The Conditions in this Part are conditions of the license grant. The rights granted in Part A are granted *only on condition* that the Work is not used in a manner prohibited by this Part. Use in violation of this Part is outside the scope of the grant and may constitute both infringement and breach **to the extent the law of the relevant forum so permits**, read together with §C.0 and §1.5.

### C.6 Construction of the Conditions

Carried forward unchanged: the Conditions are construed reasonably and in good faith by reference to the Foundational Principles; they target *purpose-built misuse* and *knowing facilitation*; the knowledge standard is **actual knowledge or willful blindness**, not negligence; ordinary lawful good-faith use is never a breach.

Carried forward unchanged: if a court holds a particular *application* of a Condition unenforceable, that holding does not affect other applications or the remaining Conditions, which continue in full force; Conditions are enforced to the maximum extent permitted by law.

### C.8 Financial Functionality — Express Boundary — *[New, Critique 3]*

To eliminate any contradiction between the *Riba* norm (§1.3(iv)) and the use of the Work to provide Financial Functionality, the following govern and prevail over any contrary reading:

**C.8.1 General permission.** The provision, operation, and ordinary lawful use of Financial Functionality — including the issuance and management of currency, ledgers, payment and settlement systems, exchanges, markets, custody, accounting, lending, borrowing, credit, and interest-, yield-, or demurrage-bearing instruments — is **expressly permitted** under this License and is **not** a breach of any Condition of this Part.

**C.8.2 The only financial limits.** Financial Functionality is reached by this Part **solely** where its use independently and squarely falls within one of the *generally applicable* Conditions §C.2–§C.5 — that is, where the financial activity is used to **defraud** (a *Sidq* breach under §C.2), to inflict **unlawful harm** (§C.3), as an instrument of **oppression or exploitation of vulnerable persons** (§C.4, e.g. predatory schemes designed to entrap), or to **betray a trust** such as misappropriating custodied funds (§C.5). No financial activity is prohibited by this License *merely* because it bears interest, yield, demurrage, or profit, or merely because some interpretive tradition might characterize it as *riba*.

**C.8.3 Interpretive supremacy of permission.** In any case of doubt regarding Financial Functionality, the interpretation favoring permission prevails (§1.3(iv)). The *Riba* norm shall never be invoked to declare a financial Work or its ordinary lawful operation non-compliant. The only operative financial constraints are the fraud, harm, exploitation-of-the-vulnerable, and breach-of-trust limits identified in §C.8.2, each of which restates conduct already independently wrongful.

A party may create a derivative, profile, or child license built upon this License (a "Derived License"), subject to the following, which protect *Sidq* and *Amanah*:

**(a)** A Derived License must adopt the Foundational Principles (§1.2) and must not weaken the Ethical-Use Conditions (Part C) below the floor set here; it may add further conditions consistent with the Foundational Principles, and may add domain-specific terms (for example, terms specific to a particular class of system) not contemplated by this License.
