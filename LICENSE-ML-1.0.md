## PART 0 — DECLARATION AND CHARACTER OF THIS INSTRUMENT

### 0.1 Nature of the Document

Maher's License ("this License", "ML-1.0", or "the License") is a single legal instrument that performs three distinct functions. First, it is a **direct license** capable of governing software, source code, documentation, datasets, designs, and other expressive or functional works ("the Work"). Second, it is an **ethical-source license**, meaning it places conditions on the *use* of the Work that go beyond the conditions found in conventional permissive or copyleft licenses. Third, and most distinctively, it is a **meta-license**: a top-level framework that defines an objective rubric by which *other* licenses may be evaluated, and classified as Compatible, Conditionally Compatible, or Incompatible with the ethical foundation set out herein.

This three-fold character is deliberate. The drafters recognize that the global software-licensing ecosystem already contains a large number of well-drafted licenses, and that the proliferation of yet more single-purpose licenses imposes a real cost on the community in the form of legal-review burden, compatibility uncertainty, and tooling complexity. ML-1.0 does not seek merely to add one more license to that landscape. It seeks to provide a *normative layer above* the landscape — a principled standard against which existing and future licenses can be measured — while remaining usable as a working license in its own right.

### 0.2 Honest Labeling (a requirement, not a disclaimer)

In keeping with the principle of **Sidq** (honesty and truthfulness), which is foundational to this instrument and elaborated below, the drafters state plainly and without euphemism: **this License is not an "Open Source" license as that term is defined by the Open Source Initiative ("OSI"), and it is not a "Free Software" license as that term is defined by the Free Software Foundation ("FSF").** It is not, and is not intended to be, approved by either body. The reason is structural and is disclosed openly: this License imposes conditions on the fields and purposes of use of the Work (see Part C), and both the Open Source Definition (criteria 5 and 6) and the Free Software Definition (Freedom 0) prohibit licenses that restrict use by persons, groups, or fields of endeavor. A license that conditions use on ethical compliance cannot, by definition, satisfy those criteria.

The drafters consider this incompatibility to be a feature rather than a defect. They further consider that *concealing* this incompatibility — by marketing the License as "open source" when it is not — would itself be a violation of **Sidq**, and therefore self-contradictory. Any party who reproduces, references, or relies upon this License is required to describe it accurately as an **ethical-source license** and a **meta-license**, and must not represent it as OSI-approved or FSF-approved unless and until such approval is in fact granted in writing by the respective body.

### 0.3 Source-Available and Ethical-Source Classification

For the avoidance of doubt, ML-1.0 is best understood as occupying the category of **source-available, ethical-source licenses**, alongside instruments such as the various ethical-source family licenses, but distinguished from them by (a) its explicit grounding in a named ethical-legal tradition, and (b) its meta-licensing function. The License nonetheless preserves and guarantees core liberties commonly associated with open collaboration — the freedom to read, study, modify, and redistribute the source form of the Work — subject only to the ethical conditions in Part C and the reciprocity conditions in Part B. The drafters' intent is that the License be *maximally permissive within ethical bounds*: every freedom that can be granted without enabling clear harm or dishonesty is granted.

### 1.1 Purpose of the Preamble

### 1.2 The Four Foundational Principles

This License rests upon four foundational principles drawn from the Islamic ethical-legal tradition. These principles are presented here as universal moral norms, intelligible and acceptable to persons of any faith or of none. The drafters do not require any user of the Work to hold any particular religious belief; they require only conduct consistent with the following norms.

**(a) Amanah — Trustworthiness and Faithful Stewardship.** *Amanah* denotes that which is held in trust, and the duty to discharge that trust faithfully. In the context of this License, *Amanah* governs the relationship between those who create a Work, those who maintain it, and those who use it. It establishes that software and data placed in one's care — including the credentials, personal information, funds, and reliance of others — must be safeguarded and not misappropriated, abused, or betrayed. *Amanah* is the principle most directly engaged by the security, privacy, and anti-exploitation conditions of this License.

**(b) Sidq — Honesty and Truthfulness.** *Sidq* denotes truthfulness in word and representation. In licensing terms, *Sidq* requires accurate attribution, honest description of modifications, truthful labeling of the License's own legal character (see Section 0.2), and the prohibition of using the Work to deceive, defraud, or misinform. A system built to deceive is, under this License, a misuse of the Work regardless of its technical sophistication.

**(c) Adl — Justice, Fairness, and Proportionality.** *Adl* denotes justice and the giving of each party its due. In licensing terms, *Adl* requires that the terms binding licensor and licensee be balanced and not structurally exploitative; that remedies be proportionate to breaches rather than punitive; that cure periods be reasonable; and that no party be placed, by the mere operation of the License, in a position of unjust advantage over another. *Adl* is the principle that disciplines the License's enforcement and termination mechanics.

**(d) Shafafiyah — Transparency and Openness.** *Shafafiyah* denotes openness and the absence of concealment. In licensing terms, *Shafafiyah* requires that the source form of the Work remain available for study and modification by lawful recipients; that the terms governing the Work be plainly stated; and that the basis for any Compatibility Determination issued under this License be published and reasoned rather than arbitrary. *Shafafiyah* is the principle that grounds the source-availability and meta-licensing provisions.

### 1.3 Subsidiary Ethical Norms

In addition to the four foundational principles, the following subsidiary norms inform the interpretation and application of this License. They are derived from the same tradition and are likewise presented as universal moral norms:

**(i) Prevention of Darar (harm).** The classical maxim *lā ḍarar wa-lā ḍirār* — "there shall be neither harming nor reciprocating harm" — establishes a general duty to avoid causing unlawful harm and to avoid retaliatory harm. This norm underlies the ethical-use conditions in Part C and the proportionality requirement in enforcement.

**(ii) Prohibition of Zulm (oppression and exploitation).** *Zulm* denotes oppression, injustice, and the placing of a thing other than in its rightful place. The Work may not be used as an instrument of *zulm* — including forced labor, trafficking, unlawful discrimination, or the systematic exploitation of vulnerable persons.

**(iii) Avoidance of Gharar (excessive uncertainty and deception in dealings).** *Gharar* denotes excessive uncertainty or hidden risk in a transaction. The Work may not be deployed to construct dealings whose essential terms are concealed from a counterparty in a manner that deprives them of informed consent.

**(iv) Avoidance of Riba (usurious exploitation).** *Riba* denotes unjust, exploitative gain extracted from another, classically associated with usury. The drafters recognize that the application of this norm to software is indirect; it is included as an interpretive value disfavoring the use of the Work as a direct instrument of exploitative lending designed to entrap borrowers, and is not intended to prohibit ordinary, lawful commercial or financial activity.

### 1.4 Universality and Non-Coercion of Belief

The drafters affirm the Qur'anic principle that "there is no compulsion in religion" (*lā ikrāha fī al-dīn*). Nothing in this License requires any user to adopt, profess, or practice any religion. The principles above are invoked as a coherent and named *ethical-legal source* for the License's conditions, in the same way that other ethical-source licenses invoke international human-rights instruments as their source. A user complies with this License by conforming their *conduct* to its terms, irrespective of their beliefs.

## PART D — META-LICENSE: EVALUATION AND CLASSIFICATION OF OTHER LICENSES

### D.1 Function and Authority

This Part establishes the meta-licensing function of ML-1.0: the framework by which the License Steward evaluates other licenses ("Subject Licenses") and publishes Compatibility Determinations. The authority to issue Compatibility Determinations rests with the License Steward, acting through the Registry. A Compatibility Determination is an interpretive and informational act; it classifies a Subject License but does not alter the terms of that Subject License, which remain the property and responsibility of their own stewards.

### D.2 The Compatibility Rubric

A Subject License is evaluated against the four Foundational Principles, each reduced to an operational test:

**(a) The Honesty Test (Sidq).** Does the Subject License avoid requiring or authorizing the misrepresentation of authorship or origin, and does it (where it makes claims about its own legal character) describe that character truthfully? A Subject License that compels removal of accurate attribution, or that misrepresents its own nature, fails this test.

**(b) The Transparency Test (Shafafiyah).** Does the Subject License permit lawful recipients to study the work, and does it refrain from prohibiting the disclosure of corresponding source to those entitled to it? A Subject License that forbids inspection or that prohibits lawful disclosure of source fails this test. (A license that simply does not *require* source disclosure — as permissive licenses do not — does not thereby fail; failure requires an affirmative prohibition.)

**(c) The Justice Test (Adl).** Are the Subject License's terms balanced, such that they do not structurally place the licensor in an exploitative or unjustly favored position over licensees, and are its remedies proportionate? A Subject License whose terms are structurally one-sided to the point of exploitation fails this test.

**(d) The Trust and Non-Harm Test (Amanah / Darar).** Does the Subject License refrain from affirmatively authorizing or compelling uses that Part C prohibits? A Subject License that *requires* conduct prohibited by Part C fails this test. (A license that is merely *silent* on ethical use — as most conventional licenses are — does not automatically fail; see the classification scheme below.)

### D.3 Classification Scheme

On the basis of the rubric, each Subject License is classified as one of the following:

**(i) ML-Compatible.** The Subject License passes all four tests, and either contains its own ethical-use conditions consistent with Part C or can be combined with the Work such that Part C continues to govern the combined whole. A Work under an ML-Compatible Subject License may be combined with a Work under ML-1.0, provided the combined distribution as a whole carries forward the Ethical-Use Conditions of Part C (see Section B.4 and B.5).

**(ii) ML-Conditionally-Compatible.** The Subject License passes the Honesty, Transparency, and Justice tests but is *silent* on ethical use (it neither authorizes nor prohibits Part C conduct). The great majority of conventional permissive and copyleft licenses fall here. A Work under such a Subject License may be combined with a Work under ML-1.0 *only* on the condition that the resulting combined distribution is offered under ML-1.0 or under terms that preserve Part C, so that the ethical floor is not lost in combination. Standing alone, such a Subject License is neither endorsed nor condemned by this License; it is simply ethically incomplete by the standard of ML-1.0.

**(iii) ML-Incompatible.** The Subject License fails one or more of the four tests — for example, by compelling misrepresentation of origin (fails Sidq), by prohibiting lawful source inspection (fails Shafafiyah), by being structurally exploitative (fails Adl), or by affirmatively authorizing or requiring Part C conduct (fails Amanah). A Work under ML-1.0 may not be relicensed under, nor have its ethical conditions subordinated to, an ML-Incompatible Subject License.

### D.4 Procedure for Compatibility Determinations (Application of Shafafiyah and Adl)

Compatibility Determinations shall be made by a transparent and reasoned procedure:
